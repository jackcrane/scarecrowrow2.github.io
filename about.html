About Scarecrow Row

Scarecrow Row is a fun fall installation of unique and creative scarecrows set along the 2-mile bike and walking trail of Otto Armleder Park, located on Cincinnati’s eastside next to Lunken Airport and Playfield.

We ask individuals, organizations and businesses to submit one of a kind, themed scarecrows, that will amuse and entertain visitors throughout the month of October.

Scarecrow Row is a proud supporter of Madi’s House, a relapse prevention and mental health community center, serving young adults battling addiction and mental health illness in the greater Cincinnati area.

For each scarecrow that is submitted we are hoping to raise $250 for Madi’s House. You can donate individually, or create a team to collectively raise the money. If you form a team, don’t forget to name it so we can track your donations, and give you the credit you deserve. We need you to help make Scarecrow Row for Madi’s House a success, so that we can best serve the young adults who battle their illness every day.

However, we do know that it’s been a tough year for some. If you only want to build a scarecrow, we would gladly accept it. You can also just give what you can.
